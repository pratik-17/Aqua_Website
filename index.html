Sharks are the ocean's most refined predators — a lineage older than trees, older than dinosaurs. Every aspect of their biology is honed by 450 million years of relentless natural selection.

Their electroreception can detect a heartbeat from meters away. Their skin is composed of dermal denticles — tooth-like scales that reduce drag and silence their approach.
